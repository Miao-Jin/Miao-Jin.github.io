Objectives
Theory and applications of discrete (digital) 3D mesh processing, focusing on representations and processing of polygonal geometric models, and applications in computer graphics.
Topics include:
  • Half edge data structures for meshes
  • Differential geometry on surfaces
  • Computational topology on surfaces
  • Mesh parameterization and its applications
  • Reconstruction
  • Simplification and multi-resolution representation for efficient rendering
  • subdivision
  • Smoothing / Fairing
  • Mesh deformation and morphing
  • Mesh editing
  • Remeshing
The lectures will cover the theoretical aspects, and provide hands-on experience.

Prerequisites
CMPS 415 or CMPS/EECE 515 Computer Graphics (strongly recommended). Students should be familiar with C++ programming.
