Objectives
This course provides a comprehensive coverage of modern computer algorithms, aiming at in-depth treatment of algorithmic design and analysis with elementary explanation while keeping mathematical rigor.

Upon completion of this course, students will be able to do the following:

  • Demonstrate a familiarity with major algorithms and data structures.
  • Analyze the asymptotic performance of algorithms.
  • Write rigorous correctness proofs for algorithms.
  • Apply important algorithmic design paradigms and methods of analysis.
  • Synthesize efficient algorithms in common engineering design situations.


References
"Introduction to Algorithms", Thomas H. Cormen, Charles E. Leiserson, Ronald L. Rivest, and Clifford Stein, 3rd Edition, The MIT Press, 2009.
