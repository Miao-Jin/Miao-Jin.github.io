Computing General Geometric Structures on Surfaces Using Ricci Flow

M. Jin, F. Luo, and X. Gu

Proceedings of ACM Symposium of Solid & Physical Modeling (SPM), pp. 61-72, 2007.

Computer-Aided Design (CAD), Vol. 39, No. 8, pp. 663-675, 2007. (invited)

Abstract: Surfaces have natural geometric structures, such as spherical structure, affine structure, projective structure, hyperbolic structure and conformal structure. Different geometric structures allow different geometries which study the invariants under the corresponding transformation groups, to be defined on various manifolds, therefore planar algorithms based on these geometries can be defined on surfaces straightforwardly. Computing the general geometric structures on surfaces has been a long lasting open problem. We thoroughly explain both theoretic and practical aspects of the computational methodology for geometric structures based on Ricci flow.
